Why Acharya exists

The hallucination problem, in the country least equipped to catch it.

Single-LLM tutors fail silently

  • One model, one voice, one chance to be wrong — confidently.
  • A Class 7 child cannot fact-check a chatbot. Neither can their parent.
  • Vernacular questions amplify the hallucination rate.

What Acharya does differently

  • Three independent LLMs run in parallel, not in a chain.
  • A judge model scores fact-by-fact agreement.
  • Brooks–Iyengar fusion discards outliers; verdict is shown in every reply.

The pedigree

Why Brooks–Iyengar?

Introduced by Dr. S. S. Iyengar and Richard Brooks in 1996 as a fault-tolerant fusion algorithm for distributed sensor networks — tolerating a minority of faulty sensors while fusing the rest into a defensible reading.

We treat each LLM as a sensor. The fusion step is structurally identical: tolerate the minority, fuse the majority, surface the disagreement.
